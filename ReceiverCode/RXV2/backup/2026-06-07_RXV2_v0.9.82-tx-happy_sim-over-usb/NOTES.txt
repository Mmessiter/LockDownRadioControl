WHAT THIS DAY ADDED: "Drive simulator over USB"
The receiver can now drive a PC flight sim (neXt / RX2SIM) as a USB HID joystick,
fed straight from the channels it receives over RF — no separate LDRC2SIM dongle
(it's the same XIAO ESP32-S3 chip).

Highlights:
- TinyUSB on the S3 builds (ARDUINO_USB_MODE=0, platform pinned espressif32@6.6.0);
  a no-op on the C3 prototype. New src/SimUsb.h holds the HID joystick + mapping.
- Home-page "Drive simulator over USB" toggle (reboots to apply). In sim mode:
  a big "SIMULATOR MODE ON" banner, only the relevant buttons shown, and the
  flight-controller output (D6) DISABLED so a real model can't be flown.
- /map "Remap channels" page: 8 outputs <- any received channel + reverse,
  NVS-persisted; 8 live 60fps-smoothed bars of the channels in use. The RX2SIM
  baseline (throttle/rudder swap + rudder reverse) is hidden, so the default
  map reads as a tidy 1:1.
- WiFi self-heal: NET_AP was a terminal dead-end; now retries home WiFi every
  20 s when AP-only with saved creds, so it rejoins on its own.
- Keep-the-TX-happy: the home page's firmware-update check did a BLOCKING
  manifest fetch in the single-threaded loop, starving radioPoll() and dropping
  the transmitter; it's now skipped while the TX link is live (LINK_LIVE_MS).

Live on the receiver PT1.local (192.168.1.231). Deploy:

  PLATFORMIO_UPLOAD_PORT=PT1.local CHIP_HOST=PT1.local dev/uploadfs_safe.sh  (web assets)
